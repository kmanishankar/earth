Earth: A Living World

What is Earth?

Earth is the third planet from the Sun and the fifth largest planet in the solar system in terms of size and mass. Its near-surface environments are the only places in the universe known to harbour life.

Interesting Facts

Basic Planet Facts

  • Earth is the 3rd planet from the Sun.
  • It’s the only known planet to support life.
  • Earth is around 4.5 billion years old.

Size and Shape

  • Earth’s diameter is about 12,742 km.
  • It is not a perfect sphere—it's an oblate spheroid, slightly flattened at the poles.
  • Earth’s surface is 71% water and 29% land.

Rotation and Orbit

  • Earth rotates on its axis once every 24 hours—causing day and night.
  • It takes 365.25 days to orbit the Sun—creating a year.
  • Earth is tilted at 23.5°, which causes seasons

Natural Wonders

  • Earth has volcanoes, mountains, oceans, deserts, and rainforests.
  • The Pacific Ocean is the largest ocean.
  • The Mount Everest is the highest point above sea level.

Atmosphere and Climate

  • The atmosphere is made of 78% nitrogen, 21% oxygen, and trace gases.
  • It protects us from harmful UV rays and meteors.
  • The ozone layer in the stratosphere absorbs harmful solar radiation.

Life and Ecosystems

  • Earth supports millions of species including humans, animals, plants, and microbes.
  • It has various ecosystems: forests, oceans, tundras, grasslands, and deserts.
  • Plants make life possible by producing oxygen through photosynthesis.
